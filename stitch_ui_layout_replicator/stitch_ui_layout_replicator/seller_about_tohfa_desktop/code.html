Our Story: The Soul of Soil

My journey with clay began in the quiet corners of my grandmother's garden in Varanasi. I remember the way the earth felt between my fingers—cool, pliable, and brimming with potential. What started as childhood play evolved into a lifelong devotion to the ancient art of pottery.

At the heart of Tohfa, my studio is a sanctuary where time slows down. Each piece is hand-thrown on a traditional kick-wheel, a process that demands presence and patience. I believe that an object made by hand carries a fragment of the maker's spirit, a warmth that machine-made items can never replicate.

Inspired by the raw textures of nature and the poetic simplicity of botanical forms, my work seeks to bridge the gap between utility and art. Every glaze is mixed from scratch using natural minerals, ensuring that every bowl, vase, or platter is a unique expression of the earth it came from.

Rooted in Responsibility

We believe that beautiful things shouldn't cost the earth. Our commitment to sustainability isn't a trend; it's the foundation of our craft.

eco

Plastic-Free Packaging

All our ceramics are wrapped in honeycomb paper and shipped in recycled cardboard boxes with water-activated tape.

water_drop

Water Reclamation

Our studio utilizes a multi-stage filtration system to recycle 90% of the water used in the throwing and cleaning process.

public

Local Sourcing

We source our stoneware and porcelain clays from regional pits to minimize transportation emissions and support local geologies.
